# 1.3 - Opportunity Identification (Chase The Pain)

Framework: Company of One - "Chase The Pain". Pain-first brainstorming only. Solutions come later.

## Troy 1.1 guardrail - Pain or Enjoyment?

Every opportunity is either solving a **pain** or providing **enjoyment**. Troy's bias: pain beats enjoyment for solo founders because pain pulls customers to pay, enjoyment has to beat every other form of fun in the world. If you still want an Enjoyment opportunity, keep it but be conscious of the harder road.

The table below has a required `Kind` column with exactly one of:

- `P` = Pain (preferred)
- `E` = Enjoyment

Minimum shortlist composition: at least 70 % `P`. If fewer, go broader before advancing.

## Troy 1.2 guardrail - Skill Fit (Venn)

Your odds of success correlate with how many of the key skills for a business you already own. The `Skill Fit (1-5)` column forces you to cross-check each pain against `SKILLSET.md`:

- 5 = 3+ key skills already `Good`
- 4 = 2 key skills `Good`, 1 `Acceptable`
- 3 = 1 key skill `Good`, 1-2 `Acceptable`
- 2 = key skills are `Acceptable` at best
- 1 = most key skills are `Bad`; would require co-founder

## Method

1. For every entry in `SKILLSET.md` sections 1, 3, 4, list pains (or enjoyment gaps) you have personally witnessed or experienced in that context.
2. Score each using the rubric below.
3. Only opportunities with composite >= 12 AND Skill Fit >= 3 advance to research.

## Scoring rubric (each 1-5)

- **Severity**: how much does the pain hurt? (Or how strong is the enjoyment desire?)
- **Frequency**: how often does it recur?
- **Willingness-to-pay**: is money already changing hands for workarounds?
- **Reachability**: can you reach 100 of these sufferers in the next 14 days?

Composite = Severity + Frequency + WTP + Reachability (max 20).
